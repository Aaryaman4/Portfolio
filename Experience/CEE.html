Organization Overview

The Centre for Environment Education (CEE) is a nationally and internationally recognized institution dedicated to environmental awareness, education, and sustainable development.

Established in 1984 as a Centre of Excellence under the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, Government of India, CEE plays a pivotal role in developing innovative environmental education programs and materials. The organization is committed to building capacity in sustainable development through field projects that demonstrate the practical application of environmental education.

With a presence across India and international collaborations, CEE works to strengthen environmental education and education for sustainable development through partnerships with government, NGOs, industry, and educational institutions.

The Plastic Tide Turners Challenge

During my internship, I was primarily involved with the Plastic Tide Turners Challenge, a global initiative backed by the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) to combat plastic pollution. This youth-led movement aims to engage and empower young people to take action against plastic waste through awareness, community engagement, and advocacy for policy changes.

Global Impact

Since its launch in 2018, the Tide Turners Challenge has mobilized over 800,000 young people across 50 countries worldwide, creating a significant impact in addressing plastic pollution. In India, CEE and WWF-India serve as key implementing partners, driving both digital and on-ground activities to tackle single-use plastics.

Challenge Structure

The Tide Turners Plastic Challenge is structured as a multi-level program that progressively engages participants in more complex and impactful activities:

Entry Level

  • Personal plastic consumption awareness
  • Individual plastic reduction pledges
  • Learning about plastic pollution impacts
  • Basic lifestyle changes implementation

Leader Level

  • Community engagement activities
  • Local awareness campaigns
  • Small-scale cleanup efforts
  • Engaging family and friends

Champion Level

  • Policy advocacy initiatives
  • Large-scale community projects
  • Institutional plastic reduction
  • Sustainable alternatives promotion
